Site Redesign

Client: The Hope Collaborative

Making Getting Involved Easier!

The Hope Collaborative, a non-profit organization, aims to serve underfunded communities and reduce food deserts by providing knowledge of agriculture and access to organic produce. The organization depends on the involvement of community members through donations and volunteer hours. This involvement from the community is essential to the organization and its mission. Unfortunately, their website did not make getting involved easy.

The Problem

With communities to feed and a mission to complete, their website fell behind over the years. Missing clear call to actions for users, major users flows buried in the global navigation and no clear information architecture, it was evident their website needed some changes. Nikia Woods, the Director of The Hope Collaborative, goal for the project was to bring the website into the future and increase user satisfaction and involvement.

The Solution

After weeks of research, our solution was to redesign the homepage and two major user flows  (donation and volunteer) and integrate secondary and tertiary user flows in the future. The new design aims to reduce the number of clicks for user to complete major actions, reduce cognitive fatigue by presenting less information and enable scrolling, and reorganize the information architecture using the LATCH technique.


My contributions: UX/UI evaluation, competitor analysis, survey writing, data evaluation, user interviews, feature ideation, interaction designs, donation user flow screen designs.

3310 Mobile

Client: The Garibaldi Company

Make Connecting With Property Residents Easy!

The Garibaldi Company is a family-owned rental property management group that thrives itself on strong resident satisfaction, superior customer service, and amazing staff. With over 15+ properties, 100+ specially trained staff, and 20,000 residents, the company is growing every day. It wasn’t until the mandated COVID-19 restrictions were in place that the once primarily face-to-face business was forced to rely on digital and online methods to interact with potential and current residents.

The Problem

As the restrictions began, their offices were forced to close to the public for what was thought to only be a short time but turned into two long years. Through survey and interviews with residents from 3310 Apartment Homes, we found that residents grew increasingly frustrated with their solely virtual connection between them and the property staff. Completing tasks such as submitting work orders, accessing rental documentation and reviewing rent breakdown became more difficult.

The resident portal was not actively updated regularly prior to the offices closing and was not able to address the residents’ needs. 85% of survey respondents did not find the portal to be useful. The portal lacked major user tasks, had broken or incomplete pages, and had poor mobile viewing. The Garibaldi Company was ready to make change and rebuild their connection with their residents through strong online resources.

The Solution

We switched back and forth between redesigning the resident portal or creating a mobile application. We ultimately decided an app would address more of the residents needs and greatly improve resident satisfaction. Residents, like Marisol, needed a centralized location that allows them to complete major tasks outside of the office hours.


My contributions: UX/UI evaluation, stakeholder and user interviews, survey writing, copy production, persona creation, interview planning, UX writing, screen designs for the issue, resources, documents and contact user flows.
